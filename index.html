About Me

Research Profile

Dedicated to advancing veterinary medicine through innovative research on host-pathogen interactions

I am a Professor and PhD Supervisor at the College of Animal Science and Veterinary Medicine, Shenyang Agricultural University. My research focuses on understanding the molecular mechanisms of host-pathogen interactions, particularly in parasitic diseases.

I have been honored with several prestigious awards including the "Young Veterinary Talent" by the Ministry of Agriculture and Rural Affairs, and recognition as a high-level talent in Shenyang City.

With over 25 SCI publications as first or corresponding author, including 17 papers in Q1 TOP journals with a total impact factor exceeding 300, my research has been published in renowned journals such as Signal Transduction and Targeted Therapy (IF: 52.7), Nature Metabolism (IF: 20.8), and Nature Communications (IF: 16.7).

Research Areas

Research Focus

Exploring the intricate relationships between pathogens and their hosts to develop novel therapeutic strategies

Host-Pathogen Interactions

Investigating the molecular mechanisms by which parasitic pathogens interact with and manipulate host cells, focusing on immune evasion strategies and cellular signaling pathways.

Immunomodulatory Drugs

Studying the immunomodulatory effects of anti-parasitic drugs to enhance host immune responses and develop more effective treatment protocols.

Parasite Biology

Comprehensive research on major parasitic pathogens including Plasmodium (malaria), Toxoplasma gondii, and Trypanosoma species.
